Glossary & Sources

Key terms collected in one place, with every source the guide cites. Figures are current as of May 2026; the supply chain moves fast, and many of these numbers will be out of date before the next election cycle.

Glossary

Frontier AI
The most capable AI systems at any given time. The EU AI Act uses a 10²⁵-FLOP training-compute threshold.
Compute
The physical chips and electricity that train and run AI. Concentrated in a few firms, which is what makes it the chokepoint.
EUV lithography
Extreme ultraviolet lithography. The technology required to manufacture sub-7nm chips. ASML in the Netherlands has a 100% monopoly on EUV machines.
CoWoS
Chip-on-wafer-on-substrate. TSMC’s advanced-packaging process that mounts GPU dies and HBM stacks together. The binding constraint on AI chip production through 2025.
HBM
High-bandwidth memory. Stacked memory chips used in every advanced AI accelerator. SK Hynix holds ~62% of the market.
Strategic dependence
In realist IR theory, when a state cannot substitute away from another state’s inputs within a politically feasible timeframe. The supplier acquires coercive leverage.
Brussels effect
Anu Bradford’s term for how the EU’s large market can force multinational firms to apply EU rules globally.
Stargate
The $500-billion, four-year AI-infrastructure venture by OpenAI, Oracle, and SoftBank announced January 21, 2025.
LAWS (Lethal Autonomous Weapons Systems)
Weapons systems that identify, select, and engage targets without human intervention in the execution. Subject to UN GGE negotiations since 2014; no binding treaty yet.
CCW (Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons)
The UN framework under which the LAWS GGE operates. Operates by consensus, which lets any single state block progress.
REAIM (Responsible AI in the Military Domain)
Summit series on military AI, hosted The Hague (2023), Seoul (2024), A Coruña (2026).
Security dilemma
The realist concept that defensive moves by one state look offensive to another, who responds in kind — producing a more militarized equilibrium than any state would choose alone.
