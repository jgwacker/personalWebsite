Ebola & the World

A virus is spreading through eastern Congo and across the border into Uganda. Stopping it isn’t just a medical problem — it’s a test of how nations choose to work together. This is your guide to both the disease and the diplomacy.

How this works

Six short sections take you from the biology of the virus all the way to the politics in Washington that shape who shows up to help. Each one is built to be read in a few minutes, with maps, timelines, and callout boxes instead of walls of text.

At the end of every section there’s a quick multiple-choice quiz — answer, get instant feedback and a short explanation, and watch your progress fill in below. When you’ve finished all six, take the Final Challenge.

Why it’s worth your time

Outbreaks are where international relations gets real. The same questions — who pays, who decides, whose problem is it — run through trade, climate, war, and refugees. Learn them here and you’ll see them everywhere.
