Glossary & Sources

Definitions of the key constructivist and AI-governance terms used throughout the guide, followed by the sources for each section. Figures current as of May 2026.

Glossary

Norm violation and norm enforcement
In constructivist IR theory, behavior counts as a norm violation only when other actors treat it as one. Enforcement — diplomatic protest, sanctions, attribution, public exposure — gives the underlying expectation its force.
Norm entrepreneur
An actor — state, NGO, religious institution, or civil-society network — that advances a new normative claim and works to convert it into binding international expectation.
C2PA
The Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity, a Linux Foundation standard for cryptographic metadata that records the origin and modification history of a digital file.
SynthID
Google's system for invisibly watermarking AI-generated content. Marks only output from Google's own models and is partially defeated by aggressive editing.
Section 230
The U.S. statute shielding online platforms from being treated as publishers of user-uploaded content. Intact in 2026; reform proposals have stalled in the 119th Congress.
Digital Services Act (DSA)
The European Union's platform-regulation regime. Opened a DSA investigation into TikTok after Romania's 2024 election annulment and fined X in December 2025.
UN OHCHR B-Tech
The UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights project, launched in 2019, applying the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights to digital technologies including AI.
European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR)
The 1950 Council of Europe treaty enforced by the European Court of Human Rights. Three judgments — Bărbulescu (2017), Big Brother Watch (2021), Glukhin (2023) — set the AI-relevant framework.
Council of Europe Framework Convention on AI
The first legally binding international AI treaty, opened for signature 5 September 2024. 37 signatories; the EU ratified on 15 May 2026. Four more ratifications required for entry into force.
Global AI Governance Initiative (GAIGI)
China's alternative international AI framework, launched October 2023. Emphasizes state sovereignty, development equity, and UN-centered governance under member-state control.
Magnifica Humanitas
Pope Leo XIV's first encyclical, signed 15 May 2026 — exactly 135 years after Leo XIII's Rerum Novarum — and released publicly on 25 May 2026.
Rerum Novarum
Pope Leo XIII's 1891 encyclical on labor and the Industrial Revolution. The founding document of modern Catholic social teaching.
CARE Principles
Indigenous-led data-governance framework published by the Global Indigenous Data Alliance in November 2020. Four principles: Collective Benefit, Authority to Control, Responsibility, Ethics.
FAIR Principles
The 2016 open-science data-sharing principles — Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable. CARE was designed as a deliberate counterpoint.
Kaitiakitanga License
Te Hiku Media's custom data license. Kaitiakitanga is a Māori concept usually translated as "guardianship." The license conditions access to Māori-language data on relationship and reciprocity with the iwi.
Sub-state sovereignty
Sovereignty claims by state-like actors — indigenous nations among them — that do not fit cleanly inside the Westphalian state system but operate increasingly as such inside the international order.
