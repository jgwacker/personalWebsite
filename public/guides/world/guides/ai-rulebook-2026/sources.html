Glossary & Sources

Definitions of the central institutionalist terms, and the full list of sources behind every claim in the guide. Figures current as of May 2026.

Glossary

Prisoner’s dilemma
A 2×2 game in which each player has incentive to defect regardless of what the other does — even though both would be better off cooperating. The canonical model for AI safety races.
Nash equilibrium
A set of strategies in which no player can do better by unilaterally changing course. A trap, not necessarily a good outcome.
Cheap talk / costly signal
“Cheap talk” = a statement that costs nothing to make or break. A “costly signal” is one that would be expensive to abandon — the test of whether a commitment is real.
Brussels effect
Anu Bradford’s term for the way the EU can globalize a rule through market access: companies face one global product instead of two.
Regime complex
A set of overlapping institutions and processes covering the same issue area, with no single authority. The AI regime complex includes the EU AI Act, the Council of Europe Convention, the OECD Principles, summits, the FMF, the AISI Network, and bilateral arrangements.
EU AI Act
The European Union’s comprehensive AI regulation, in force August 2024, phasing in through 2027. Fines tiered: €35M/7% (prohibited practices), €15M/3% (most violations including GPAI), €7.5M/1% (incorrect info).
GPAI
General-Purpose AI — the EU AI Act’s term for foundation models. Triggers Article 53 obligations including the training-data summary requirement.
Council of Europe Framework Convention on AI
The first legally binding international AI treaty. Opened for signature September 5, 2024. 37 signatories. Awaiting five ratifications (including three CoE members) to enter force.
OECD AI Principles
The most widely endorsed soft-law standard, originally adopted 2019 and updated May 2024. 47 adherent governments.
NIST AI Risk Management Framework
U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology framework (Jan 2023). Defines Govern, Map, Measure, Manage functions. Renamed home: Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) in 2025.
AI Safety Institutes (AISIs) / CAISI
National bodies launched after Bletchley (Nov 2023) to evaluate frontier models. The U.S. AISI was renamed CAISI (June 2025); the U.K. AISI was renamed AI Security Institute (Feb 2025).
Frontier Model Forum
An industry body launched in July 2023 by Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, and OpenAI. Coordinates research grants and intelligence-sharing.
