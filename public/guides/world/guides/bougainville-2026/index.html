How this works

Six short sections take you from the geography of one Melanesian island to a parliamentary vote scheduled for 30 August 2026 — and from there into a Pacific that is more militarized and more contested than at any time since the Cold War. Each section is built to be read in a few minutes, with maps, timelines, and callouts instead of walls of text.

At the end of every section there’s a quick multiple-choice quiz — answer, get instant feedback and a short explanation, and watch your progress fill in below. When you’ve finished all six, take the Final Challenge.

Why it’s worth your time

Bougainville is where the textbook concepts of international relations — self-determination, territorial integrity, recognition, free association, soft balancing — stop being abstractions. A single island is about to test all of them at once, and the answer will shape the Pacific for a generation.
