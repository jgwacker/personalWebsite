Glossary

Self-determination
The principle, recognised in UN Charter Articles 1(2) and 55, that peoples are entitled to determine their political status. Decolonisation operationalised it; the question of how it applies to internal minorities and post-colonial breakaways is still contested.
Territorial integrity
The principle, codified in UN Charter Article 2(4), that the borders of existing states are protected against forcible alteration. It binds states in their dealings with each other and does not, on its face, prohibit internal secession.
Remedial secession
A contested legal doctrine, most clearly developed by James Crawford, holding that secession may be justified where the parent state has committed gross human-rights violations and blocked internal self-determination. The ICJ declined to endorse it in the 2010 Kosovo opinion.
Decolonisation
The post-1945 process of dismantling European overseas empires. UN GA Resolution 1514 (1960) made it a duty in international law; the saltwater thesis restricted the right to geographically-separated and culturally-distinct overseas territories.
Saltwater (blue water) thesis
The doctrine that the Resolution 1514 right of self-determination applies only to territories separated from their administering power by salt water and ethnically distinct from it. It was promoted by the United States and Latin American states and effectively excluded internal indigenous peoples from the 1960s decolonisation wave.
UDI (unilateral declaration of independence)
A declaration of independence issued without parent-state consent. The ICJ's 2010 Kosovo opinion held UDIs are not prohibited by international law — but they do not, on their own, create an entitlement to recognition.
Free association
A category of constitutional arrangement, distinct from both sovereignty and autonomy, in which a smaller state runs its own domestic affairs and has its own parliament but defers defence and some foreign-policy functions to a larger partner. The Cook Islands and Niue (with New Zealand), and Marshall Islands and Palau (with the United States), are the standard examples.
Sessional order
A procedural device used by parliaments to set the terms of how a specific item is debated. The Melanesian Agreement of June 2025 commits PNG to using a sessional order to bring the Bougainville referendum result to the National Parliament in 2026.
ABG (Autonomous Bougainville Government)
The Bougainvillean government established under the Bougainville Peace Agreement of 2001. President since 2020: Ishmael Toroama, a former BRA field commander, re-elected by landslide in September 2025.
BPA (Bougainville Peace Agreement)
The August 2001 agreement that ended the 1988–98 war. Its three pillars are autonomy, weapons disposal, and a non-binding independence referendum. Section 342 of the PNG Constitution gives Parliament "final decision-making authority" over the referendum result.
Era Kone Covenant
The April 2022 agreement between Marape and Toroama, signed at Era Kone in Bougainville, that committed both governments to a window of "not before 2025, not later than 2027" for independence and to tabling the result in PNG's parliament by 2023.
Melanesian Agreement
The June 2025 agreement signed at Burnham, New Zealand. It "recognise[s] that the Bougainville desire for independence is legitimate," establishes a bipartisan PNG parliamentary committee, and commits to a sessional-order procedure for ratification in 2026.
Pukpuk Treaty
The Australia–PNG Mutual Defence Treaty signed on 6 October 2025 in Canberra. Australia's first new defence alliance in over 70 years and PNG's first ever. Includes a mutual-defence clause and a citizen-recruitment provision.
PIF (Pacific Islands Forum)
The main regional intergovernmental body for the Pacific. Eighteen members; Bougainville is not currently one. The September 2025 Honiara summit produced the Ocean of Peace Declaration.
MSG (Melanesian Spearhead Group)
The Melanesian sub-regional body. Members: Fiji, PNG, Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, and the FLNKS. Indonesia is an associate member. Bougainville has applied for observer status.
Bougival Accord
The 12 July 2025 agreement on New Caledonia's status, proposing a sui generis "State of New Caledonia" within France with devolved foreign-relations powers. The FLNKS independence movement withdrew support within a month; the implementing referendum has been delayed indefinitely.
