<h2>The Key Idea</h2>

<p>
Gluino searches at hadron colliders traditionally assumed specific SUSY spectra with long decay chains. This proceedings contribution reviews a more model-independent approach: focusing on the robust signature of jets plus missing transverse energy without requiring specific intermediate particles. The key insight is that gluinos that decay directly to quarks and a neutralino produce a distinctive jets+MET signature that can be searched for with simple kinematic variables, independent of the details of the rest of the SUSY spectrum.
</p>

<p>
The approach described here, developed in the companion papers on <a href="/research/2008-plb-directly-decaying-gluinos">directly decaying gluinos</a> and <a href="/research/2009-physrevd-jets-met">jets+MET</a>, helped establish the simplified-model paradigm that became standard practice at the LHC.
</p>
