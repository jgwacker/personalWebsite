<h2>The Key Idea</h2>

<p>
The <a href="/research/2002-jhep-ewsb-theory-space">EWSB from Theory Space</a> paper had identified a stable scalar from the discrete symmetry of the moose construction as a potential dark matter candidate. This paper does the full calculation: thermal relic abundance including all couplings to the gauge and Higgs sectors. The dark matter candidate is an admixture of an SU(2) triplet and an SU(2) singlet scalar, making it a WIMP with distinctive couplings. Two regions of parameter space give the correct relic abundance: a light region around 100 GeV and a heavy region above 500 GeV. The paper established that Little Higgs models could naturally accommodate dark matter without additional model-building, providing a competitor to the neutralino dark matter of supersymmetry.
</p>
