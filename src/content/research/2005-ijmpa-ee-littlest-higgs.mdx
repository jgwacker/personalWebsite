<h2>The Key Idea</h2>

<p>
Most phenomenological studies of Little Higgs models focus on the LHC, but lepton colliders offer complementary probes with cleaner experimental environments. This proceedings contribution examines what an electron-electron collider could reveal about the Littlest Higgs model -- specifically, the spectrum and couplings of the new gauge bosons and heavy top partner. The e-e- initial state (as opposed to e+e-) provides unique access to certain channels through t-channel exchange of the new heavy particles predicted by the Little Higgs symmetry-breaking pattern.
</p>
