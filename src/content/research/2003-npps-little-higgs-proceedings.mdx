<h2>The Key Idea</h2>

<p>
Little Higgs models address the hierarchy problem by making the Higgs a pseudo-Goldstone boson of a spontaneously broken approximate global symmetry. The crucial ingredient is collective symmetry breaking: no single interaction explicitly breaks the symmetry that protects the Higgs mass, so one-loop quadratic divergences are cancelled by partners of the same spin. This proceedings contribution summarizes the construction and key features of these models, based on the work in the <a href="/research/2002-jhep-minimal-moose">Minimal Moose</a> and <a href="/research/2002-jhep-ewsb-theory-space">EWSB from Theory Space</a> papers.
</p>
